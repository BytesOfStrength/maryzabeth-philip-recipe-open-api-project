Strawberries Hugged in Chocolate

A simple and elegant treat perfect for any special occasion. These are super easy to make!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of fresh, washed and dried strawberries
  • 1 cup of chocolate or candy chocolate flavored candy wafers with a 1/2 cup extra on the side

Instructions

  1. Prepare a cookie sheet or any flat surface object that is freezer safe to use with parchment paper. You will need to work fast so chocolate doesn't cool too quickly.
  2. Melt your chocolate over a double-boiler or in the microwave using 30second intervals at 50% power level or use a chocolate candy melter machine. You want the chocolate to just be mostly melted. If it's too runny, add a little extra chocolate until it is smooth.
  3. Stir until smooth and cool down so thickness is not runny but looks like a thick cake batter consistency.
  4. Pour your melted chocolate in small deep bowl enough to fit the bottom half of the strawberry.
  5. Hand dip each strawberry into the chocolate until it is 1/2 way covered and pull directly out and let it rest on the parchment lined cookie sheet
  6. After you have placed as many chocolate dipped strawberries on your cookie sheet as you can in a single layer, place sheet in freezer for 10 minutes or until firm or use fridge to firm up chocolate.
  7. Pack up your chocolate in air tight containers and place in fridge until ready to use.
  8. If you are planning to serve them in a warmer climate, keep in fridge and then a few minutes just until serving move finished strawberries to room temperature.
  9. As long as your strawberries are not overripe, eat within 2 days after making them, otherwise very ripe strawberries may mean you need to eat them the same day.Remember to follow food safey rules with any perishable items.

Sponge Cake Leftover Trifle

You made homemade spongecake, but now you have leftovers. An easy way to use it is to make a trifle in individual cups.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 inch to 1 inch cut up spongecake or cake of your choice
  • 1 cup of very cold heavy whipping cream
  • 2 tablespoons of sugar
  • pre-made pudding cups. flavor of your choices
  • pre-frozen cut up mixed fruit or use ready made fruit cups drained of juice

Instructions

  1. Depending on how much spongecake, you have leftover, you may have enough to fill a larger dish and serve from one large container or only enough to fill about 2 or 3 small fruit goblets. Your goal is to make fairly even layers of each of the ingredients. Feel free to change up proportions
  2. Make whipped topping. Whip up your whipping cream with sugar. You can use a handmixer or do this manually with a balloon whisk. You want just barely firm peaks so the top of the cream when you lift up the whisk just barely flops over. If you overmix, you will get butter. So be careful!
  3. In each of the serving dishes, layer a bottom layer of the spongecake pieces, a layer of pudding,a layer of fruit pieces, another layer of spongecake pieces, and top it off the whipped cream.
  4. This is a dessert that will not stay firm in very warm temperature so keep chilled until ready to serve. It is recommended to make and serve the same day for best results.

Cake with cookie nut butter pops

This takes on a new meaning to cake pops. Use cream cheese and sweetened nutbutter or hazelnut spread to bind the cake instead of frosting.

Ingredients

  • pre-made pound cake. about 16oz cut up in 1/2 inch pieces
  • 4oz of cream cheese softened
  • 4 oz of nut butter, hazelnut spread, or those fancy nut butters with cookie pieces/li>
  • melted chocolate or candy coating made as per manufactuers directions

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine by hand all the ingredients until it is uniform in texture.
  2. Using a cookie scoop, scoop uniform balls and smooth edge in your palms. Place these on a parchment lined cookie sheet.
  3. Freeze the balls for about 5 minutes or until firm
  4. While the balls are freezing prepare your melted chocolate or candy coating and cool so it's smooth but not runny
  5. Either dip the firm balls using a fork to dip them and place on a single layer on cookie sheet or stick a candy pop stick about 1/2 way into ball and then dip into chocolate.
  6. While chocolate has still not set, sprinkle any colorful sprinkles or crushed topping of your choice on the cake pops
  7. Firm up cake pops in fridge or freezer just until chocolate is set.
  8. Serve and Enjoy!

Note on Food Safety

Baking and eating the results of a recipe are enjoyable, but food safety is a top priority, especially in warm and humid environments! Remember these important tips to keep your baked goods and tasty food fresh and safe to eat:

  • ***Keep it Cool:*** Any desserts with fresh cream, whipped toppings, or perishable fillings should be kept refrigerated at safe temperatures right up until serving. You can find safe temperature guidelines presented by the Food Safety division of the government.
  • ***Watch the Chocolate:*** Chocolate can melt in warm environments, so keep any chocolate dipped treats and frostings in a cool place.
  • Follow all recommended food safety guidelines as instructed by foodsafety.gov or CDC
